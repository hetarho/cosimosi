# 25. latent star field

> latent star field — a **rendering-only** field of gray "latent-neuron" points scattered through the hippocampus
> z-band, plus the **awaken** animation that fires when a real neuron is created. The gray field is the visual metaphor
> for the not-yet-recruited / silent engram; it has **no DB rows** — only *activated* neurons are persisted.
> Scope: FE | Status: planning
>
> Mounts inside [23.universe-canvas](23.universe-canvas.md) as a background layer *behind* the real stars/neurons
> ([24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md)), consuming the [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md)
> boundary. Instanced ([ARCHITECTURE §3.3](../ARCHITECTURE.md)), authored in TSL. Purely presentation — no server,
> no schema.

## Purpose

The empty universe must not read as *nothing*. From the first login — before a single diary is written — a faint field
of gray points drifts through the lower (hippocampus) region of the space ([V7], PRD §4.1). These are **latent
neurons**: the visual metaphor for engram cells that exist but have **not yet been recruited** into a memory — the
"silent engram" the product is built on ([F2], PRD §2, PRD §5.3.5). They carry no meaning: no brightness dynamics, no
emotion color, no name, no identity.

When the user launches a diary and a genuinely new neuron is created (Encode / [20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md);
LaunchStars in [27.writing-flow-ui](27.writing-flow-ui.md)), one of those gray points **awakens** — an animation that
hands the eye off from "background dust" to a real `cell-star` ([24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md)).
This is the visible half of the neuroscience the engine implements headlessly: **competitive, excitability-based neural
allocation** — a recently active neuron is more excitable, so a nearby latent cell is the likely one to be recruited
next ([E7], [E7a], Han 2007). The animation reads as "a silent cell lighting up near its neighbours."

The two jobs of this unit — **draw the gray field** and **choreograph the awakening** — are pure presentation. They
give the universe a living, populated backdrop and make neuron birth legible, without inventing any domain state.

## Scope / Non-goals

**In scope**
- **`entities/latent-star` — a rendering background layer.** A field of roughly `rendering.latent_star_count` gray
  points, scattered through the hippocampus z-band (`force_sim.hippocampus_z_min`..`force_sim.hippocampus_z_max`, from
  [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md)). No brightness, no color, no per-point identity. Rendered as one (or a
  few) `InstancedMesh` bucket(s) authored in TSL, mounted behind the real stars/neurons inside
  [23.universe-canvas](23.universe-canvas.md). This is a **visual (rendering) entity** in FSD terms
  ([ARCHITECTURE §3.1](../ARCHITECTURE.md), §3.4) — it projects nothing from the domain mirror; it *is* ambiance.
- **`features/awaken-neuron` — the activation choreography.** On a new-neuron creation event (surfaced by the
  writing/launch flow), pick one gray latent star and play an **awaken** animation from it: the gray point brightens /
  scales / dissolves as the real `cell-star` takes over at its emergent position. One `features/<verb>` slice, one user-
  facing outcome ("a new memory element is born").
- **The competitive-allocation *entry* choreography ([E7a]).** The gray star chosen to awaken is picked **near a
  recently-active neuron** — the same conceptual time window used for temporal proximity in
  [21.link-usecase](21.link-usecase.md) ([L4]) — or a **random** latent star when there are no recent cues (e.g. the
  first-ever neuron). This "awaken near" is only the *starting point* of the animation.
- **Tunable density; mobile MVP may lower the count.** `rendering.latent_star_count` is one generated build-time scalar;
  the mobile MVP renders a reduced count (fewer instances) per the web=open-beta / mobile=MVP rule
  ([ARCHITECTURE §3.5](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).

**Non-goals**
- **No DB schema, no server, no RPC.** Latent stars are **rendering-only** and have **no rows** ([E7a], §7 of the Epic A
  brief). A `neurons` row exists **only** when a neuron is actually activated, and it is persisted by Encode /
  [20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md) — never by this unit. This unit issues no reads or writes.
- **No real neuron logic.** Extraction, dedup/normalization, activation, and synapse formation are
  [20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md) / [21.link-usecase](21.link-usecase.md). This unit only *animates* the
  moment a new neuron already decided upon by the domain appears.
- **No stored "awaken position."** The awaken anchor is a client presentation decision, not domain truth (see Design).
  The real neuron's final position is **emergent** from the force-sim and is never stored ([I5],
  [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md)).
- **No neuron/star bodies.** The real `cell-star` / `star` / `filament` bodies are
  [24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md); the camera/navigation rig and force-sim wiring are
  [23.universe-canvas](23.universe-canvas.md); the emotion nebula is [26.nebula-color-field](26.nebula-color-field.md).
- **No renderer bootstrap.** The renderer boundary, TSL toolkit, and skin seam come from
  [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md); this unit consumes them.
- No hardcoded tuning scalars in FE code (they live in `values.yaml`); shader graphs stay in code.

## Design

### The rule this unit is built on
Latent stars are a **rendering background**, not domain state. There is no `latent-star` type in the Go domain, DB,
proto, or the FE domain-mirror — it exists only as a visual (rendering) entity (PRD §3.2, §3.4). Only when a neuron is
*actually activated* does a `neurons` row exist, and that row is written by Encode
([20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md)), never here. Consequently: **the gray field is generated client-side, the
awaken is an entry animation, and no part of it is persisted or read from the server.**

### `entities/latent-star` — the gray field (visual entity)
- **Placement.** A *visual* entity slice `entities/latent-star` (FSD; [ARCHITECTURE §3.1/§3.4](../ARCHITECTURE.md)),
  segment `ui` for the R3F component + TSL bindings, `model` for the deterministic field generator, `config` for
  slice-local constants that are not tuning numbers. It imports **no** domain-mirror entity (it projects nothing) and
  no concrete renderer beyond the [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md) boundary. Visual words never
  travel upward ([ARCHITECTURE §3.4](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).
- **The field.** Roughly `rendering.latent_star_count` points, positioned once at scene init by a **deterministic,
  seeded** generator (reuse `force_sim.seed` from [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md) so web↔mobile agree and
  the field is reproducible). Each point's `z` lies in the hippocampus band
  `[force_sim.hippocampus_z_min, force_sim.hippocampus_z_max]`; `x`,`y` fill the visible hippocampus volume. Points have
  **no brightness, no color, no name, no identity** — a single uniform gray tint, small size. Slight ambient drift
  (a shader-time wobble) is allowed for a "living dust" feel but carries no meaning.
- **Rendering.** One (or a few) `InstancedMesh` bucket(s) ([ARCHITECTURE §3.3](../ARCHITECTURE.md)), material authored
  in **TSL** (so one source compiles to WGSL for WebGPU and GLSL for the WebGL2 fallback — no raw `onBeforeCompile`).
  Instance transforms are written once into `InstancedMesh.matrix`; there is no per-frame React state and no force-sim
  participation — the field is static/ambient. Mounted **behind** the real stars/neurons inside
  [23.universe-canvas](23.universe-canvas.md) (a background layer), so real bodies always render on top.
- **Independence from the sim.** Latent stars are **not** nodes in the force-sim — they are decorative and do not
  attract or repel real nodes. This keeps [I5] clean: only real neuron/synapse relationships drive layout.

### `features/awaken-neuron` — the activation animation (feature)
- **Trigger.** The **`new_neuron_ids`** returned by `LaunchStars` (the `LaunchStarsResponse` field owned by
  [20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md), surfaced by [27.writing-flow-ui](27.writing-flow-ui.md)). Newness —
  *created* vs *deduped onto an existing neuron* — is a **server-only** decision the client cannot derive, so the launch
  response carries exactly the genuinely-created neuron ids; this feature reacts to them. Dedup/reuse means a launch may
  create fewer new neurons than it references — only the ids in `new_neuron_ids` awaken. Each new neuron's emergent
  position arrives from the sim on the next `GetUniverse` read.
- **Choosing which gray star awakens (the entry choreography, [E7a]).**
  1. Determine the **anchor**: pick a *recently-active* neuron — one activated within the same conceptual time window
     used for temporal proximity ([L4], [21.link-usecase](21.link-usecase.md)). "Recently active" here is a
     **client-side presentation heuristic** over the visible graph, not a server fact.
  2. Select the latent star **nearest that anchor** as the awaken **seed** (start point). If there is **no recent cue**
     (e.g. the first-ever neuron, or no neuron activated inside the window), pick a **random** latent star.
  3. Play the awaken animation *from* that seed to the real neuron's target position.
- **The animation.** The chosen gray point brightens / scales up / hands off as the real `cell-star`
  ([24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md)) fades in at its **emergent target position**. Visually the
  latent star is "consumed" into the new neuron; the field's remaining count is unaffected (the field is replenished
  conceptually — the point simply stops being drawn as latent). Timing/easing live in the animation code (a shader
  graph / motion curve = code, not a tuning value).
- **What is a presentation decision vs. domain truth — explicit.**
  - **Presentation (this unit):** *which* gray star awakens, *where* the animation starts, and the "awaken near a
    recent neuron" behavior. None of it is stored or sent to the server.
  - **Domain truth (elsewhere):** *that* a new neuron exists ([20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md)) and *where it
    settles* — its **final position is emergent** from force-sim relationships ([I5],
    [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md)), never a saved coordinate. "Awaken near" is only the animation's
    starting anchor; the neuron then moves to wherever its synapses pull it.
- **State.** Awaken is a short, transient animation lifecycle (idle → awakening → done) — modeled as slice `model`
  control-state if it needs discrete phases (XState per [ARCHITECTURE §3.2](../ARCHITECTURE.md)), reading positions per
  frame via `getSnapshot()` / refs, never 60fps React state. Multiple simultaneous births (a launch creates several new
  neurons at once) each get an awaken; the picks must not collide (each new neuron consumes a distinct latent star).

### Mobile (React Native)
- Same slices, shared verbatim; the TSL material and field generator are platform-pure and reused. The MVP renders a
  **reduced `rendering.latent_star_count`** and may simplify the ambient drift ([ARCHITECTURE §3.5](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).
  A `*.native.tsx` sibling exists only if the R3F host wiring genuinely differs; the field/animation logic does not fork.

### Deferred / left open
- The exact **awaken animation vocabulary** (brighten vs. scale vs. dissolve, duration, easing) is a code/motion choice
  tuned during implementation, not a plan decision — **left open (see root DECISIONS.md)**.
- **Latent-star drift** (whether the gray points wobble, and how much) is **left open (see root DECISIONS.md)**; the
  default is a subtle, meaning-free ambient drift. It aligns with the representational-drift direction but is not
  required for this unit.
- The precise **"recently active" window and nearest-star metric** on the client — conceptually the [L4] window — is
  **left open (see root DECISIONS.md)**; the concrete client heuristic is deferred to implementation.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Rendering-only, no DB.** The latent-star field is generated and drawn entirely on the client; it produces **no**
   database rows, no RPC calls, and no server state. A `neurons` row exists only for an *activated* neuron, and it is
   written by Encode ([20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md)), not by this unit ([E7a]).
2. **Field density & placement.** On scene init the universe shows roughly `rendering.latent_star_count` gray points,
   each with **no brightness, no color, and no identity**, positioned within the hippocampus z-band
   `[force_sim.hippocampus_z_min, force_sim.hippocampus_z_max]` (from [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md)) and
   filling the visible hippocampus volume in x,y.
3. **Background layer.** The field renders as a background layer inside [23.universe-canvas](23.universe-canvas.md),
   *behind* the real stars/neurons/synapses of [24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md); real bodies
   always render on top.
4. **Awaken on new-neuron creation.** When a launch creates a new neuron
   ([20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md)/[27.writing-flow-ui](27.writing-flow-ui.md)), exactly one gray latent star
   plays an **awaken** animation and hands off to the real `cell-star`. Multiple new neurons in one launch each awaken a
   distinct latent star.
5. **Awaken anchor = near a recent neuron, else random.** The gray star chosen to awaken is the one nearest a
   *recently-active* neuron (the [L4] temporal window used conceptually); when there is no recent cue the pick is
   **random**. This selection is a client presentation decision — nothing about it is persisted or sent to the server.
6. **Final position emergent, not stored.** The awaken start point is only the animation's anchor; the real neuron's
   **final position is emergent** from the force-sim ([19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md)) and is **never
   stored** ([I5]). No "awaken position" is written anywhere.
7. **Instanced + TSL.** The field is drawn with `InstancedMesh` bucket(s) and a material authored in **TSL** (compiles
   to WGSL + GLSL; no raw-GLSL `onBeforeCompile`), mounted through the [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md)
   renderer boundary — no slice imports `three` directly ([ARCHITECTURE §3.3](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).
8. **Count tunable; mobile lowers it.** `rendering.latent_star_count` is a single build-time generated scalar
   (`values.yaml` → `pnpm gen:values`); changing it changes the field density with no code edits, and the mobile MVP
   renders a reduced count ([ARCHITECTURE §3.5](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).
9. **Emergent stays rendered-not-modeled.** The unit adds **no** `latent-star` type to the Go domain, DB, proto, or the
   FE domain-mirror; visual words do not travel upward into the domain slices ([ARCHITECTURE §3.4](../ARCHITECTURE.md)).

## Policy / Values Impact

- **tech/** — once implemented, the rule **"latent stars are rendering-only (no DB); the awaken is entry choreography;
  the seed anchor is a client presentation choice; the real neuron's final position is emergent and never stored"** is
  recorded in the as-built rendering rules ([tech/rendering.md](../tech/rendering.md), extended at `/implement-job` —
  **not pre-written now**). The latent-star field's shader graph and the awaken motion curve are **code** (like the skin
  preset graphs of plan 14), not `values.yaml` content.
- **policy/ux** — reinforces [V7]/[F2]: the empty universe is never truly empty (a silent-engram backdrop from first
  login), and neuron birth is made legible by the awaken effect. No new invariant is introduced; the unit consumes
  existing ones ([I5], [I1]/[F2]). Lands in `policy/ux` on implement (not pre-written).
- **spec/values.yaml** (enumerated now; generated at implement via `pnpm gen:values`):
  - `rendering.latent_star_count` — the approximate number of gray latent-neuron points in the field ([E7a], [V7]); a
    single scalar, lowered for the mobile MVP.
  - *(Excluded from values — code/content:* the latent-star TSL material graph and the awaken animation curve/easing
    (shader/motion graphs = code); the deterministic field-generator formula. Reused, not re-declared here:
    `force_sim.hippocampus_z_min` / `force_sim.hippocampus_z_max` and `force_sim.seed` are owned by
    [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md); the renderer/instancing tunables (`rendering.max_pixel_ratio`,
    `rendering.instance_bucket_size`, `rendering.active_skin`) are owned by [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md).)
- **Dependencies:** [14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md) (renderer boundary · TSL toolkit · skin seam),
  [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md) (hippocampus z-band bounds · seed · emergent positions),

  [20.encode-usecase](20.encode-usecase.md) (the new-neuron creation event that triggers the awaken; the only writer of
  `neurons`) and [24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md) (the real `cell-star` the awaken hands off to).
