# 57. 3D assets and background design

> 3D assets and background design — the product's visual identity for the universe: star shape/material, cell-star /
> filament / latent-field / nebula aesthetics, background/skin/atmosphere, the emotion palette **values**, and the
> visual design of 3D states (dim/gist/awaken) & choreography. Scope: FE | Status: planning
>
> This is a **design plan**, the 3D sibling of [56.2d-ui-design-language](56.2d-ui-design-language.md). Like 56 it is
> *designer-reviewed* and completed through **continuous iterative feedback** — its Definition of Done is a
> design-reviewer sign-off against a rubric across review rounds, not a single test pass (see Acceptance Criteria). Its
> durable output is the 3D section of the shared living guideline ([tech/design-language.md](../tech/design-language.md),
> created when the job completes) — the SSOT every 3D presentation unit cites.

## Purpose

[14.rendering-foundation](14.rendering-foundation.md) built the rendering **mechanism** — `@cosimosi/3d-renderer` (the
TSL shader-art toolkit, composable skins + skin seam, R3F scene layers, the asset-source port, the canvas host) — and
Epic A's [24.star-neuron-rendering](24.star-neuron-rendering.md) / [25.latent-star-field](25.latent-star-field.md) /
[26.nebula-color-field](26.nebula-color-field.md) built functional bodies (`@cosimosi/universe-render`: `StarLayer`,
`CellStarLayer`, `FilamentLayer`, `LatentStarField`, `NebulaField`, `AwakenNeuron`) against **first-pass, placeholder
visuals** — enough to prove the pipeline, not a designed identity. As plan 09 is to 2D, plan 14 owns *how the universe
renders*, not *what it looks like*.

The universe's actual visual identity has never been designed. **The star's shape** — its silhouette, material, and
how it reads as "a memory" — was drawn just well enough to validate rendering; [24](24.star-neuron-rendering.md) now
explicitly disclaims owning it. Every forthcoming 3D unit ([39.forgetting-visuals](39.forgetting-visuals.md),
[42.gist-star-rendering](42.gist-star-rendering.md)) and choreography ([31.time-acceleration-ui](31.time-acceleration-ui.md),
[41.consolidation-usecase](41.consolidation-usecase.md)) will layer new visual states on undesigned bodies unless the
identity exists first. This plan authors the **3D visual identity**: the star's form, the other bodies' aesthetics,
the universe background/skin/atmosphere, the **emotion palette values**, and the visual design of the 3D states — and
captures it in the shared living guideline so it is designed once, coherently, against the full known visual
vocabulary (PRD [V1]–[V9]).

Together with [56](56.2d-ui-design-language.md) (the DOM identity) it makes the product read as one thing.

## Scope / Non-goals

**In scope** — the visual identity of the 3D universe:

- **Star form** — the episodic-memory star's shape/silhouette, material, brightness/size expression, and seed-form
  variation (the visual design behind plan 24's body).
- **Other bodies** — cell-star (neuron), synapse filament, latent-field, and nebula aesthetics.
- **Universe background** — skin(s), atmosphere, lighting, depth cueing, and z-layer (hippocampus/neocortex)
  readability, expressed through plan 14's skin seam.
- **Emotion palette values** — the redesigned default `mood → color` aesthetic (the actual color values). Owned here
  (the universe is where emotion-color is most load-bearing — nebula, star color); **referenced by** plan 56 so 2D
  emotion accents match. Applied via plan 17's mapping *structure*, not replacing it.
- **3D visual states — design only** — the *look* of forgetting dimming/word-loss, gist rising, and neuron awaken.
  (The *mechanics* — when/how much — stay in their functional plans: 37/39, 40/42, 25.)
- **3D motion / choreography feel** — the *visual design* of the time-acceleration and consolidation choreography.
  (The *mechanics/triggers* stay in 30/31/41.)
- **The living guideline** — [tech/design-language.md](../tech/design-language.md) (3D section) at job completion,
  plus a **reference universe showcase scene** rendering every body type, state, skin, and the emotion spectrum on
  web + mobile — the substrate the designer reviews each round.
- **Retrofit** — the shipped Epic A 3D bodies are re-designed to the new identity as the first application and proof.

**Non-goals**

- **All 2D DOM chrome** — typography, component look, panels/HUD/modals, 2D motion, layout →
  [56.2d-ui-design-language](56.2d-ui-design-language.md).
- **The rendering *mechanism*** — the TSL toolkit, skin seam, asset-source port, scene-layer plumbing, canvas host,
  and the instancing/performance architecture → plan 14. This plan restyles *within* that mechanism and must not
  regress it.
- **Layout / positions** — node coordinates are emergent from [19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md) and never
  stored ([I5]); this plan designs how bodies *look*, never where they *sit*.
- **The `mood → color` mapping structure & swap mechanism** — domain, owned by [17.emotion-model](17.emotion-model.md)
  and [51.palette-customization](51.palette-customization.md). This plan owns the *values*, not the mapping or the
  meaning layer ([I11]).
- **The functional mechanics of forgetting / gist / awaken / choreography** — owned by 37/39/40/42/25/30/31/41; this
  plan owns only their *visual design*.
- **Domain data, the universe clock, any non-presentation state.**

## Design

### What the 3D language defines

The 3D identity is the set of decisions that make the universe read as one designed place: the **star's form** and
material, the aesthetics of cell-stars / filaments / latent field / nebula, the **background/skin/atmosphere** and
depth/z-layer legibility, the **emotion palette values**, the **visual design of the 3D states** (dim/gist/awaken),
and the **choreography feel** (acceleration, consolidation). It is authored as the 3D section of
[tech/design-language.md](../tech/design-language.md) and demonstrated on the reference universe showcase scene.

### Architecture placement — expressed through the render packages, positions untouched

Visual changes are expressed inside the render packages: skins / assets / shader-art in `@cosimosi/3d-renderer`, and
the body layers in `@cosimosi/universe-render` — never as per-scene hardcode in an app. Web and React Native stay at
parity via the shared renderer (`ARCHITECTURE` §3.5). **Positions remain emergent** from
[19.force-sim-layout](19.force-sim-layout.md) — this plan designs appearance only and sets no coordinates ([I5]). The
plan-14 rendering mechanism (skin seam, asset-source port, instancing) is used, not modified.

### Emotion palette values — one source, both sides

The redesigned default `mood → color` **values** live here as the palette source, consumed in 3D (nebula, star color)
through plan 17's mapping structure and **referenced by plan 56** for 2D emotion accents — so the emotion identity is
one aesthetic across the whole product. Palette *table content* stays in code/asset (not `spec/values.yaml`, per the
plan 09 / [11.config-values](11.config-values.md) rule that color-table content is not generated numeric tuning); the
mapping structure and the user-swap remain plan 17 / 51.

### The review substrate — a reference universe showcase scene

A design plan's proof is a rendered surface. This plan produces a **reference universe showcase scene** that renders,
on web + mobile: every body type (star / cell-star / filament / latent / nebula), every designed state (default /
dimming / gist-risen / awakening), the available skin(s)/background, and the full emotion-color spectrum. It is the
artifact the design reviewer inspects each round, built on plan 14's canvas + the `/test` surface rather than a
parallel one.

### The iterative designer-review process

Completion is **not** a single sequential pass. Work proceeds in **review rounds**, identical in shape to plan 56:

1. Apply/adjust the identity in the render packages + the showcase scene.
2. The **design reviewer** inspects the scene and scores each **rubric dimension** — *star/asset form & silhouette ·
   material/lighting · emotion-color legibility · depth & z-layer readability · state legibility (dim/gist/awaken) ·
   motion/choreography feel · atmosphere/background cohesion · performance-within-budget* — as **Meets / Needs-work /
   Blocking**, with notes.
3. Notes go into a **feedback ledger** tracked to `resolved` / `deferred` (deferred items listed explicitly).
4. Repeat until **sign-off**: every rubric dimension **Meets** and **zero open Blocking**.

### Retrofit as first proof, within the performance floor

The shipped Epic A bodies are re-designed to the identity as the first real application. Throughout, plan 14's
**performance floors hold** — instancing, the frame budget, and TSL-based rendering — and the web + RN parity path
stays intact; a beautiful star that breaks the frame budget or the instancing model fails the rubric's
performance-within-budget dimension.

## Acceptance Criteria

> Two tiers. **Tier 1 (items 1–7)** is objective and checkable. **Tier 2 (items 8–10)** is the iterative
> designer-review gate that defines "done" for a design plan — sign-off against a rubric across rounds, not one pass.

1. **(Tier 1 · objective)** A living guideline exists ([tech/design-language.md](../tech/design-language.md), 3D
   section) documenting: star form & material, the other bodies' aesthetics, background/skin/atmosphere & z-layer
   depth, the emotion palette values, the 3D state visual design (dim/gist/awaken), and the choreography feel.
2. A reference universe showcase scene renders every body type, every designed state, the skin(s)/background, and the
   full emotion-color spectrum on **web and mobile**.
3. All visual changes are expressed through `@cosimosi/3d-renderer` + `@cosimosi/universe-render` (+ the palette
   source); no per-scene hardcode, and node **positions remain emergent** from force-sim (never set here) ([I5]).
4. The emotion palette values are authored as one source, consumed by 3D (nebula/star color) and referenced by plan 56
   for 2D — the emotion aesthetic matches across 2D and 3D.
5. The shipped Epic A 3D bodies are retrofitted to the new identity.
6. Plan-14 performance floors are preserved (instancing, frame budget, TSL rendering); the web + React Native parity
   path stays intact.
7. Presentation-only — no domain, clock, layout, or meaning mutation; positions and the meaning layer untouched.
8. **(Tier 2 · iterative designer-review gate)** The 3D rubric (star/asset form & silhouette · material/lighting ·
   emotion-color legibility · depth & z-layer readability · state legibility · motion/choreography feel ·
   atmosphere/background cohesion · performance-within-budget) is scored by the design reviewer each round as Meets /
   Needs-work / Blocking.
9. A feedback ledger records every round's notes, each tracked to `resolved` or `deferred` (deferred items listed
   explicitly).
10. **Definition of Done:** the design reviewer signs off — every rubric dimension **Meets** and **zero open
    Blocking** — recorded as a dated sign-off. Completion is sign-off-gated and iterative; there is no single
    sequential pass.
