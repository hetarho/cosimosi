## Implementation notes (as-built decisions and limitations)

- **Checker design.** A bounded tokenizer + paren-grouping + scope-walk implementation (no SQL parser dependency).
  Ownership rule: every product relation in a statement must reach an external parameter through `user_id` equality
  links collected in WHERE/ON/HAVING contexts. Documented, deliberate limitations â€” all fail-CLOSED, never open:
  an OR/NOT disjunct never proves scope (a genuinely-scoped disjunctive query must be allowlisted or rewritten);
  a CTE referenced through a join does not transfer its internal scope to other relations; bare `user_id` in a
  multi-relation scope, duplicate real-table aliases, set-returning functions in FROM, and unsupported statement
  kinds are violations. `FILTER (WHERE â€¦)` predicates are ignored (they restrict aggregates, not row reads).
- **Allowlist.** `memory/jobs.sql#ClaimDueJob` and `#PurgeTerminalJobs` (worker queue scans, global by design) and
  `twinkle/ledger.sql#TwinkleLedgerDedupExists` (payment dedup keys are deliberately unique across users; its
  `user_id = arg OR payment` shape is exactly the disjunct the OR rule refuses).
- **R016 attribution correction.** The review-time palette `act(...)`/Timeout findings were already resolved by
  job 71's App.test rework. The remaining worker force-exit had a different true owner: test QueryClients whose
  reads were still failing/retrying at suite teardown settled afterwards and scheduled `defaultGcMs` (5-minute) gc
  timers on orphaned queries. Fixed at the owner: test-context clients are `retry: false` + `gcTime: Infinity`
  (TanStack's documented testing configuration), universe-mounting App tests get a transport that implements
  `GetUniverse`, and render handles unmount before `dispose()`. No force-exit flag, no warning suppression, and the
  suite dropped from ~9â€“21 s (plus a silent 5-minute worker hold) to ~4â€“6 s.
- **Diary wait.** The waits are already authoritative-condition-based (`waitFor` on rendered message keys); with the
  retry/gc fix no flake or slow-drain remains across repeated runs. The documented 20 s `testTimeout` headroom for
  CI CPU starvation is kept as-is.
